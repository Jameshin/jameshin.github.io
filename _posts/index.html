Please do not waste your time

Blessed is the man that walketh not in the counsel of the ungodly,
nor standeth in the way of sinners, nor sitteth in the seat of the scornful.
But his delight is in the law of the Lord; and in his law doth he meditate day and night.

Please, do not waste your time trying to wash away what you perceive as my shortcomings. God is at work in the very life you are living now, at the level you find yourself today. Scripture tells us that He has already planted treasure within that place—within your present reality—and assures us not to be anxious.

The insistence that “the church must grow” or “we must achieve victory” is unnecessary. Such language often springs from a desire for power, reward, or authority, and it is from that craving that words like “revival” and “triumph” emerge.

But the truth is simpler: God’s work is not bound to our striving for status or success. It unfolds quietly, faithfully, in the midst of our ordinary lives.
